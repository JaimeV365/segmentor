Getting started with segmentor.app

This guide walks you through everything you need to start segmenting your customers. No account required—your data stays on your device.

In this guide: Upload your data → Understand your segments → Chart controls → Filters → Analyse results → Export and save. Time to complete: 10–15 minutes.

1. Upload your data

The main upload screen offers three ways to get data into segmentor.app:

Option A: Upload a CSV file

Use the Upload CSV option and select your file. Headers matter: the first row of your CSV should contain column names (e.g. Satisfaction, Loyalty, Name, Email, Date). The tool uses these to detect and map columns automatically. Your CSV should include:

  • Satisfaction score (required): 1–3, 1–5, or 1–7 scale
  • Loyalty score (required): 1–5, 1–7, 1–10, or 0–10 scale
  • Customer name/ID (optional): for identifying individuals
  • Email (optional): for tracking customers over time
  • Date (optional): for historical progress
  • Other attributes (optional): country, region, product, etc.

Expected column headers (first row) and an example row (from the demo file):

IDName/EmailSatisfaction-5Loyalty-10Date(dd/mm/yyyy)
ID0093Emma Johnson1001/01/2024

In the tool, use the Download template button to get a CSV with the right structure, or download the template here. Tip: The tool normalizes different scales, so you can mix 1–5 and 1–10 in the same dataset. If the column mapping screen appears, map your columns to Satisfaction and Loyalty as prompted.

Option B: Enter data manually

Choose Manual Entry and fill in the form for each customer. Satisfaction and loyalty scales are mandatory; all other fields (name, email, date, etc.) are optional. If both email and ID are left blank, the row is accepted but treated as anonymous. Best for small datasets or quick tests.

Option C: Load project

Choose Load Project to open a previously saved .seg file. Your data, midpoint, filters, and settings are restored exactly as you left them. How to save a .seg file is explained in Exporting and saving (section 6 below).

New to the tool? You can also try demo mode or use sample data from the tool to explore before uploading your own. Once your data is in, your customers appear automatically in the four quadrants. Next, make sense of those segments.

2. Understanding your segments

Your customers are divided into four segments based on satisfaction and loyalty. The main chart shows all four quadrants. For more detail on the model, see the FAQ: What is the Apostles Model?

Loyalists (top right)

High satisfaction + high loyalty. Your best customers—they like you and stay. The extreme top-right corner is the Advocates sub-zone: superfans who promote you. What to do: Maintain their experience, ask for referrals, feature them in case studies. More in FAQ

Hostages (top left)

Low satisfaction + high loyalty. Unhappy but locked in (switching costs, contracts, lack of alternatives). They'll leave when a better option appears—and may tell others why. What to do: Urgent intervention. Find out why they're dissatisfied and fix it. More in FAQ

Mercenaries (bottom right)

High satisfaction + low loyalty. Happy with you but not emotionally attached; they'll switch for a better deal. What to do: Build loyalty through rewards, community, or switching barriers. More in FAQ

Defectors (bottom left)

Low satisfaction + low loyalty. High churn risk. The extreme bottom-left is the Trolls sub-zone: they may damage your brand through negative word-of-mouth. What to do: Win back if possible, or let them go gracefully. More in FAQ

The midpoint

The midpoint (where the four quadrants meet) determines how customers are classified. You can drag it to reposition quadrant boundaries. If your data clusters in certain areas, moving the midpoint can create more meaningful segments. Manual reassignments for boundary cases are cleared if you move the midpoint and the point is no longer on the line.

3. Chart controls

Use the chart controls to customise how the quadrants and data are displayed.

Terminology

Switch between Classic (Apostles, Terrorists) and Modern (Advocates, Trolls) labels. The segmentation logic is the same—only the names change.

Zone display

Show or hide Near-Advocates zones, the Advocates sub-zone, and the Trolls sub-zone. Use these to focus on the extreme corners of the chart.

Labels

Set label options: all labels (quadrants + zones), quadrants only, or no labels.

Grid and axes

Toggle grid lines, scale numbers, and axis legends on or off.

Clicking data points

Click any point on the chart to see customer name/ID, satisfaction and loyalty scores, quadrant assignment, and other attributes. For overlapping points, the tool shows frequency; click again to see all customers at that position.

Manual reassignment (boundary cases)

If a customer sits exactly on a quadrant boundary, you can manually reassign them to an adjacent quadrant. Use this when business context suggests they belong in a specific segment. Reassignments are cleared if you move the midpoint and the point is no longer on the boundary.

4. Using filters

Filters let you focus on a subset of your data. By default, when you add filters to the main chart, they apply to all reports too. You can disconnect them by modifying filters locally (per report) if you want a report to use different filter criteria.

What you can filter by

Filter by date (e.g. last 7/30/90 days, custom range, or specific date), by attributes (country, region, language, or any column from your CSV), and by scores (satisfaction or loyalty range).

Connecting and disconnecting filters

You can combine multiple filters—for example, "UK customers from Q1 2024 with satisfaction below 5." Each filter you add narrows the data further. To remove a filter, clear it or disconnect it; the chart and reports then include the rows that were excluded by that filter. Clear all filters to return to the full dataset.

5. Analysing your results

Open the Reports section from the left menu. Segmentor offers two main report types plus supporting views.

Data Report

Shows overall statistics (averages, distributions), quadrant breakdown (counts and percentages), response concentration (where customers cluster), and—if your data has dates and repeated customers—historical progress. Use it to understand the composition of your customer base. Click Generate Data Report in the Reporting section.

Action Plan

Provides strategic recommendations per segment, proximity analysis (at-risk customers near boundaries), conversion opportunities, and ROI-prioritized actions. Use it to decide what to do next. Click Generate Action Plan in the Reporting section. You'll need to accept a short disclaimer before viewing (it contains strategic recommendations, not just data).

Proximity analysis

Identifies customers near quadrant boundaries who could move between segments with small changes. These are high-leverage opportunities—e.g. a Mercenary close to the Loyalist boundary may need only a small loyalty boost to become a long-term customer.

Response concentration

Shows where most responses actually cluster (the mode), not just the average. Averages can be pulled by outliers; concentration shows where customers really sit.

Historical progress (if available)

Requires a Date column and the same customers (by email or ID) at multiple dates. You'll see how averages change over time, individual movements between segments, multi-movement journeys, and which transitions are most common. Use it to track improvement efforts and spot trends.

6. Exporting and saving your work

You can save your project and export reports in several formats.

Save your project (.seg file)

Use the Save button in the left menu to export a .seg file. It stores your data, midpoint position, zone settings, manual reassignments, filters, and other settings. Save to your computer; reload later with Load and select the file. The file is saved directly to your device—never uploaded to our servers.

Export reports

From each report section you can export PDF (Data Report, Action Plan, or combined), Excel/CSV (raw data with segment assignments, historical progress, proximity lists), and PNG (chart and visualizations). Use the export option in the report you're viewing; files download to your computer.

Customising exports (premium)

Free reports include segmentor.app branding. Premium users can remove branding and add their own logo, colours, and branded reports for client presentations. In the tool: Controls → Watermark → Watermark Controls to upload a logo and adjust size, position, and transparency.

Sharing with colleagues

Share .seg project files so others can load and continue the analysis; share PDF reports for viewing without the tool; or share exported CSV data for use in other tools. All processing is local—you control what you share.
